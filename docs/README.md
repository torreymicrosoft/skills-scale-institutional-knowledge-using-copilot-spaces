## Overview of Project Management Processes

OctoAcme follows an iterative, customer-first approach to project delivery. Every project begins with a structured **initiation** phase where the business need is validated, stakeholders are identified, success criteria are defined, and a go/no-go decision is made. A lightweight one-pager, stakeholder list, and initial risk register are produced before any planning work begins.

During **planning**, the approved initiative is decomposed into a prioritized backlog with clear acceptance criteria, effort estimates, dependencies, and a milestone map. The team aligns on a Definition of Done and a release plan before execution starts. Clear **roles** are assigned throughout â€” a Project Manager coordinates delivery and risk, a Product Manager owns outcomes and prioritization, developers implement and test, and QA validates quality and acceptance criteria.

**Execution and tracking** rely on a daily standup rhythm, a GitHub Projects board (Backlog â†’ Done), and a weekly delivery sync. Pull requests are kept small, linked to issues, and require automated CI checks plus at least one peer approval before merging. **Risk management and communication** are ongoing: a risk register is maintained and reviewed each week, stakeholders receive regular status updates, and escalation paths are defined upfront.

After every sprint, release, or significant milestone the team holds a **retrospective** to capture what went well, what could improve, and 2â€“3 prioritized action items. Validated learnings feed back into documentation and process updates, supporting continuous improvement. **Releases** follow a standardized checklist â€” all acceptance criteria met, CI passing, release notes drafted, and a rollback plan in place â€” before any deployment to production.
